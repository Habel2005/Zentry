The TIST Library plays a vital role in supporting the academic mission of the institution by identifying and fulfilling the information needs of its diverse user community. The college maintains a centrally located Central Library which, along with the MBA Library, Digital Library, and 10 department-specific reference libraries, forms a robust library network accessible to students and faculty.
The Vivekananda Central Library, occupying 10,000 sq. ft., is fully automated and offers a comprehensive suite of academic resources and services. Library services are organized under the following units:
Vivekananda Central Library
Sarabhai Digital Library
C.K. Prahalad MBA Library
Department Reference Libraries
All operations are centrally managed by the Vivekananda Central Library under an open-access system. The collection is organized using the Dewey Decimal Classification System, and the entire library is computerized using KOHA, a web-based integrated library management software. The Online Public Access Catalogue (OPAC) and Web OPAC allow users to search, access, and manage their library transactions (issue, renewal, overdue alerts) through personalized accounts.
Each user is issued a bar-coded ID card. Access control systems, Hands-free barcode scanners for Students and Staffs, are installed at the entrance. Surveillance cameras ensure a secure and regulated environment.
A well- arranged Digital Library equipped with 25 computer terminals, projector, screen, and audio system, provides access to a wide range of digital academic content including:
NPTEL Video Lectures
E-Journals and E-Books from platforms such as IEEE, ScienceDirect, EBSCO, Knimbus, DELNET, and the National Digital Library of India (NDLI)
More than 9,425 international e-books through EBSCO
These resources are accessible both on-campus and remotely via IP authentication and mobile platforms. Previous year question papers, seminar reports and project reports are available any where in the campus.
The library also subscribes to Turnitin plagiarism detection software to support research integrity.
The NAAC peer team that inspected the library facilities and services rated them as “Excellent”.

Feedback to AICTE
Toc H International was founded during the First World War, by Rev. Tubby Clayton, an army Chaplain, in Poperinge, Belgium. Talbot House, as the house was called – or Toc H in signaler’s code for ‘T’ and ‘H’ – provided a haven of rest and refuge for the wounded and sick soldiers.
The Toc H movement in India began in Kochi in 1972. In the initial stages, it extended humanitarian and charitable services to leprosy and TB patients in various sanatoria and helped inmates of orphanages, mentally challenged children, and the down-trodden. Later on, Toc H entered the educational field and is presently running numerous schools and Toc H Institute Of Science & Technology, Arakunnam.
We, at TIST, anticipate an educational environment that incorporates many novelties in the curriculum, within the University prescribed parameters. Interdisciplinary collaborations are encouraged in projects and the laboratories are equipped with ultra-modern amenities, to cater to the emerging needs of industries. Social commitment is met by offering scholarships to deserving, economically backward rural students. Creative content is given priority while adopting pedagogy models like Bloom’s Taxonomy and Creative Learning Process.
The Quality of our learning environment got recognized when the college was accredited by NAAC – the only college at that time to get this honor under CUSAT. Our academic performance is excellent when compared to Government Colleges which have the advantage of subsidized education. Five B. Tech programs for TIST have NBA Accreditation (NBA 2018) a coveted achievement. This accredited status makes it possible for our students to get B. Tech(Hons.) degree to satisfy other eligibility conditions.
TIST provides seven M.Tech programs, in addition to a full-time MBA program. An entirely new Research Wing has been established under the banner of: J.C. Bose Center for Research and Advanced Studies. The college has 15 researchers with Ph.D. research in areas like Thin Film Technology, LASER, Environmental Sciences, Energy Conservation, Waste Management, Electronics & Communication Engineering, Data Security, etc.
A separate Department of Applied Psychology and Counseling-, a unique initiative- has been established to take care of the overall mental well-being of the students. The students are also encouraged to participate in sports and games such as basketball, football, cricket, tennis, and so on, for which extensive facilities have been provided. An in-door stadium takes care of such activities even during the monsoon.
The college is now looking forward to synergizing the activities of all disciplines in order to motivate the students to cross the barriers perceived by many, namely seeking jobs, under the umbrella of their specialized branches. A sense of entrepreneurship is also instilled in the students by a separate wing fostering Creativity and Innovation, namely IEDC
The campus placement status of a batch that is just graduating is excellent with more than 360 more job offers, an increase of over 60%, compared to the previous years. There are several core companies like Ashok Leyland, Keynes Interconnection Systems, Cherytech India, Kwangjin India and so on which have placed our students.
At the moment, TIST is incubating more productive engineers, researchers, and job creators than mere job seekers, in the advancing field of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Management studies.

The Department of Civil Engineering, TIST was established on 2006. The Department offers both Under Graduate and Graduate courses. UG course is B.Tech in Civil Engineering and Graduate course is M.Tech in Construction Engineering and Management. The department now comprises of 16 highly qualified teaching staffs & 07 non teaching staff guiding Undergraduate and Postgraduate students.The faculty of the department continues to strive hard by conquering new frontiers of knowledge through quality research work, QIP and FDP programs for enhancing the Teaching Learning process. The faculty moulds the students as change agents by imparting the latest developments in technology and research areas taking into account professionalism, ethics and sustainability in addition to curriculum.  The department is in close association with Institution of Engineers (IE), Indian Society for Technical Education (ISTE) and has an active student chapter of Indian Concrete Institute (ICI), and Indian Green Building Council ( IGBC), and ASCE students chapter.
The Department has a very strong consultancy division catering for both in-house and external projects and also has several MoU’s signed with CAFET INNOVA Technical Society (CITS) Hyderabad, BASHA Research Corporation (BR Corp) Singapore, CADD Centre training Services Piravom. Toc H MBA became the First Institute in Kerala to partner with Veleuciliste U Pozegi, Croatia under the prestigious ERASMUS + Mobility programme. Erasmus stands for European Community Action Scheme for the Mobility of University Students, established in 1987. Erasmus+ is a programme run by the European Union and is managed by agencies in each EU member country, as well as by the Education, Audio-visual and Culture Executive Agency (EACEA) of the EU. The focus of this programme is on four different fields – education, training, youth and sport to improve skills and increase employability.
To formalise the partnership, Memorandum of Understanding was signed between Veleuciliste U Pozegi, Croatia and Toc H Institute of Science & Technology under the prestigious ERASMUS exchange Programme in 2018. This MoU offers exchange of faculty and students between these two institutions. Under this agreement, so far three faculty has represented India for the Erasmus+ Teaching Mobility for Academic Staff.
Dr Pearly Saira Chacko, Dr Tessla Arakal and Mrs Frank Hycinth, Assistant Professors of Toc H MBA was invited for the  Erasmus + International Teaching Mobility Programme at Veleuciliste U Pozegi, Croatia from 23rd to 27th May 2022.
